design: it has dominant black color and White triple-line stripes near the top and bottom. Thinner red and white stripes in the midsection with small diamond motifs woven within them. The lower part of the shawl is adorned with vertical rows of white floral/starburst motifs—arranged in evenly spaced columns.

description: The dominant black color represents dignity, strength, and spiritual depth—qualities highly respected in Khiamniungan culture. these lines signify layers of life and transitions, often interpreted as stages in a woman's life, and the red may represent vitality or ceremonial status. The white motifs provide a beautiful contrast and add ceremonial elegance to the garment.
